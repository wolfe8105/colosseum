COMMUNITY GUIDELINES

Last updated: February 26, 2026

THE ARENA HAS RULES

The Colosseum is built for strong opinions, heated debate, and real disagreement. That's the point. But there's a line between arguing hard and causing harm. These guidelines define that line.

1. ATTACK ARGUMENTS, NOT PEOPLE

Go after the take, not the person. "That's a terrible argument because..." is fine. "You're a terrible person because..." is not. Ad hominem attacks, slurs, threats, and personal harassment will result in immediate action.

2. NO HATE SPEECH

Content that promotes violence, discrimination, or hatred based on race, ethnicity, religion, gender, sexual orientation, disability, or national origin is prohibited. No exceptions.

3. NO DOXXING

Never share another user's personal information (real name, address, phone number, workplace, school) without their explicit consent. This is a permanent ban offense.

4. NO MANIPULATION

Don't use multiple accounts, bots, or coordinated groups to manipulate votes, predictions, or debate outcomes. Don't intentionally lose debates to boost another account's Elo.

5. KEEP IT LEGAL

Don't use the Platform to plan, promote, or facilitate illegal activity. Don't threaten violence. Don't share illegal content.

6. RESPECT THE MODERATORS

Moderators are community members who volunteer their time. Follow their instructions during debates. If you disagree with a moderation decision, use the appeals process — don't harass the moderator.

7. ENFORCEMENT

Warning → Mute (24h) → Temp Ban (7d) → Permanent Ban. Severe violations (threats, doxxing, hate speech) skip directly to temp or permanent ban.

8. THE SPIRIT

The Colosseum exists because disagreement is valuable. We want the smartest, most passionate arguments — not the loudest or cruelest. Argue like you want to convince, not like you want to destroy. The best debaters win with skill, not cheap shots.
