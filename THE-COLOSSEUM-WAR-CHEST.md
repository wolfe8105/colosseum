# 1. THE PIVOT — FREE PLATFORM, PREMIER DATA

1.1. Consumer subscriptions and token packs are **shelved at launch**. Code stays, flipped off.
1.2. The app is free. Zero gates. Email + username + you're in.
1.3. Users are the labor force. They generate data by debating, voting, predicting, reacting.
1.4. Revenue comes from B2B data licensing and ad inventory. Not from users.
1.5. Every $5 paywall you put up kills a data point worth thousands to the right buyer.
1.6. Plinko Gate drops the payment step. OAuth → Age → Username → Done.
1.7. Profile Depth feature stays (generates rich demographic data). Price gate removed or made optional premium.
1.8. Token system remains in code for future premium features — not active at launch.

# 2. WHY THE DATA IS PREMIER

2.1. **What already exists:** polling data (Gallup, Pew, YouGov), social sentiment (Brandwatch, Sprout Social), prediction markets (Polymarket, Kalshi).
2.2. **What none of them have:** structured, real-time, head-to-head argumentation with outcomes.
2.3. Polls = what people SAY they think. Social media = what people POST. Prediction markets = what people BET.
2.4. The Colosseum = what people are willing to FIGHT for, which arguments actually LAND, and which side WINS when both show up.
2.5. This is persuasion dynamics, not opinions. Nobody else collects it in this format.
2.6. The data is behavioral — zero surveys. 250 data items mapped across 25 buyer industries (analytics layer, Session 33).
2.7. 18 RPCs wired to log_event(). Pipeline is already running.

# 3. PRICING PSYCHOLOGY

3.1. People don't find value until someone else finds value. Price signals quality.
3.2. At $25K you're a trial. Easy to approve, easy to cancel. Low commitment = low usage.
3.3. At $100K+ you're a real line item. Someone has to justify the spend. They'll actually log in.
3.4. At $500K+ you're a strategic partner. Board meeting. War room. The signer's career is on the line — which means they USE it.
3.5. The $10M pitch (year 2-3): you're not handing them data, you're running their argument R&D. Embedded intelligence operation.
3.6. **Year 1 reality:** you're an unproven dataset from an unknown platform. Floor price is $25K-$50K. That's where 40% of alt data subscriptions live (Neudata). Earn your way up from there.
3.7. **Year 2-3 target:** proven data, repeat customers, exclusivity premiums. That's when $100K-$500K+ contracts become real.
3.8. Don't confuse the aspirational ceiling with the launch floor. Sell $25K subscriptions first. Prove value. Then raise prices.

# 4. THREE REVENUE TIERS

4.1. **Tier 1 — Open Intelligence ($25K–$100K/year)**
4.1.1. Non-exclusive. Anyone can buy.
4.1.2. Historical data, trend reports, broad topics.
4.1.3. Buyers: think tanks, media companies, brands, market research firms, political consulting firms.
4.1.4. They get value but no competitive edge over a direct rival.
4.1.5. **This is year 1 pricing.** 40% of alt data subscriptions sell at $25K/year or less. Entry-level for an unproven dataset.
4.1.6. Comparable: a single national poll costs $30K. One focus group session = $10K-$12K. Your annual sub replaces multiple polls/groups with continuous data.

4.2. **Tier 2 — Competitive Intelligence ($100K–$500K/year)**
4.2.1. Semi-exclusive by industry vertical.
4.2.2. One hedge fund per strategy type. One PR firm per market. One brand per category.
4.2.3. Coca-Cola gets it, Pepsi doesn't.
4.2.4. Buyer knows their direct competitor is locked out.
4.2.5. **This is year 2 pricing.** Requires proven data volume and at least one reference customer. Comparable enterprise sentiment tools (Brandwatch, Kapiche) charge $10K-$32K/year without exclusivity — exclusivity premium justifies the multiplier.

4.3. **Tier 3 — Wartime Exclusivity ($500K–$2M+/year per cycle)**
4.3.1. One side of a head-to-head fight gets full, real-time access. Other side gets nothing.
4.3.2. Custom topic tracking. Dedicated analyst briefings. First look at emerging trends.
4.3.3. Political campaigns, litigation wars, pharma battles.
4.3.4. Both sides know the clock is ticking — first to sign locks out the other.
4.3.5. **This is year 2-3 pricing.** Requires statistical significance in data volume and a proven track record. The average alt data dataset generates $1.1M/year (Neudata) but that's skewed by top performers. Earning your way here takes time.

4.4. **Tier math (realistic):**
4.4.1. Year 1: 1-5 Tier 1 customers at $25K-$100K = $25K-$500K.
4.4.2. Year 2: 5-10 mixed Tier 1/Tier 2 = $250K-$1.5M. First exclusivity premium possible.
4.4.3. Year 3: 10-20 customers across all tiers + auction premiums = $1M-$10M ceiling.
4.4.4. These are NOT guarantees. These are backed-by-research ranges. See Section 16 for full projections.

# 5. THE AUCTION MODEL

5.1. Both sides of any fight know the data exists. Both know the other side wants it.
5.2. Whoever gets it has radar. Whoever doesn't is flying blind.
5.3. You don't run the auction openly. You let both sides know the other is interested. Human nature does the rest.
5.4. The losing bidder doesn't walk away — they buy Tier 1 (open intelligence) as consolation.
5.5. You monetize the loser twice: once as leverage to drive up the exclusive price, again as a consolation buyer.

# 6. THE 45-DAY PITCH (VERBATIM — DO NOT POLISH)

> "Hey, look. Here are your 10 competitors. Someone's gonna buy this information. The clock starts today. 45 days, someone's gonna buy it. I'm never gonna tell you who bought it. And I'm never gonna tell you for how much. All you're gonna know is on that day, you lose because you don't have it."

6.1. This is loss aversion. People hate losing more than they love winning (Kahneman, Nobel Prize).
6.2. The clock kills deliberation. Fear moves faster than committees.
6.3. The secrecy is permanent. They'll never confirm or deny if their competitor has the data.
6.4. Every future loss — campaign, case, product launch — they'll wonder if the other side had the data.
6.5. After the first 45-day window closes and someone buys, the next window opens. Day-one calls start happening.

# 7. STEALTH POSITIONING

7.1. "You won't find anything on us because we don't want to exist."
7.2. Invisibility protects data integrity — observation changes behavior. If users know they're being mined, they perform differently. Data gets contaminated.
7.3. Same reason Nielsen households weren't supposed to know. Same reason focus groups use two-way mirrors.
7.4. The Colosseum (consumer app) is loud, public, spectacle. Bot army drives traffic. Everyone sees the arena.
7.5. The B2B intelligence operation has no website, no LinkedIn, no press, no logo. Referral-only. Closed-door meetings.
7.6. Two entities in one body: the public gladiator show and the private intelligence firm watching from the emperor's box.

# 8. PRE-SELLING DATA FUTURES

8.1. Sell the harvest before planting the field. Futures contracts for data.
8.2. "In 90 days, this platform will have thousands of debates on the topics you care about. One of your competitors is going to own that data."
8.3. Buyer pays a deposit / reservation / first-right-of-access upfront.
8.4. Use that capital to fund growth: better bot army, paid promotion, seed debates on buyer's topics.
8.5. 90 days later, deliver.
8.6. **Risk:** if you take money and don't deliver, you're done in that industry. Word travels.
8.7. **Mitigation:** first presale goes to a safer buyer (think tank, market research firm) on broad topics where organic volume is guaranteed. Prove the model. Then hunt whales.

# 15. DEFENSE NOTES

15.1. If users learn they're being mined, behavior changes. Data integrity dies. Stealth is a defense mechanism, not just branding.
15.2. Data access controls (tier-based) are Ring 1 in the castle model. Not started, not needed until first sale approaches.
15.3. Pre-selling creates a delivery obligation. Never promise what you can't generate in the window. Start conservative.
15.4. Exclusivity contracts need legal review before signing. Non-compete clauses, NDA structure, delivery guarantees.
15.5. One bad delivery = burned in that entire industry. Word travels in political/legal/finance circles.
